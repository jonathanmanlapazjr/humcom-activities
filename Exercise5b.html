SINIGANG

Description
-is a Filipino soup or stew characterized by its sour and savoury taste.
-It is most often associated with tamarind (Filipino: sampalok), although it can use other sour fruits and leaves as the souring agent.
-Sinigang is most often associated with tamarind in modern times, but it originally referred to any meat or seafood cooked in a sour and acidic broth.
-Sinigang typically use meat or seafood (e.g., fish, pork, beef, shrimp, or chicken) stewed with tamarind, tomatoes, garlic, and onions.
-Most Filipinos like to cook sinigang with green long peppers in order to enhance the taste and add a little spice to the dish.

INGREDIENTS:

  • Tamarind 9pcs
  • Red Onion 3pcs
  • Tomato 3pcs
  • Fish Sauce 5tbsp
  • Radish 2pcs
  • Kangkong 2pc
  • Pork 1kg

Preparation and Directions
